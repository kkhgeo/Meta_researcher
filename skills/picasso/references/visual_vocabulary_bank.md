# Visual Vocabulary Bank

Reference sources for the STYLE field of the universal prompt schema. Organized by emotional register rather than medium, because register is what the user describes and the skill needs to translate.

**Rule of use**: When a concept fits multiple registers, pull at least one reference from outside the obvious category. This is the anti-slop mechanism at the vocabulary level.

---

## Register 1 — Quiet melancholy, solitude, contemplation

### Cinema
- **Andrei Tarkovsky** — long takes, water, ruins, misted interiors (Stalker, Mirror, Nostalghia)
- **Béla Tarr** — black-and-white, glacial pace, weathered architecture (Werckmeister Harmonies)
- **Kelly Reichardt** — Pacific Northwest, muted palette, everyday solitude (First Cow)
- **Chantal Akerman** — domestic stillness, held frames (Jeanne Dielman)

### Painting
- **Vilhelm Hammershøi** — grey Copenhagen interiors, doorways, absent figures
- **Andrew Wyeth** — dry grass, weathered wood, Pennsylvania and Maine
- **Caspar David Friedrich** — Romantic solitude, figures facing landscapes
- **Edward Hopper** — American solitude, artificial light at night

### Photography
- **Saul Leiter** — obscured NYC street scenes through windows, fogged glass
- **Masao Yamamoto** — small, handled, worn prints of natural details
- **Rinko Kawauchi** — luminous everyday fragments

### Medium / texture
- Cyanotype, salt print, silver gelatin with age, watercolor on rough paper

### Lighting
- North-window daylight, overcast grey, single source from behind curtain

## How to deploy this bank in Phase 3

Given the decomposed concept:

1. Identify the primary register from Registers 1–7
2. Pick one cinema + one painting/photography + one medium reference from within that register
3. Pick one additional reference from the **adjacent domains** section
4. Combine them in the STYLE field — this produces specificity that single-register references cannot

Example for a concept in Register 1 (quiet melancholy):
- Cinema: Tarkovsky's Stalker
- Painting: Hammershøi interior
- Medium: north-window daylight, watercolor with visible paper grain
- Adjacent: Japanese boro textile (mended, layered)

STYLE field: "Watercolor with visible rough-paper grain, composition inspired by Hammershøi's Strandgade interiors and the misted thresholds of Tarkovsky's Stalker, with the layered mended texture of Japanese boro"
