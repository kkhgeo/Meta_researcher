# Journal Style Reference

PHASE 1에서 사용자가 저널명만 제공했을 때 로드한다.
이 파일의 프로파일로 Blueprint 차원을 근사한다.
Confidence는 Medium 또는 Low — 정확한 Blueprint를 위해 실제 논문 제공을 권장한다.

수록되지 않은 저널은 Web 검색으로 보완하거나, 사용자에게 논문 제공을 요청한다.

---

## Geochimica et Cosmochimica Acta (GCA)

- **Tone:** Highly technical, data-centric; strong passive voice preference
- **Sentences:** Long and complex; numerical values always paired with uncertainties
- **Macro logic:** Geochemical framework -> Analytical methods -> Isotopic/elemental data -> Interpretation
- **Signature phrases:** "The results suggest...", "Isotopic compositions indicate...", "consistent with..."
- **Citations:** (Author et al., year); frequent figure and table cross-references
- **Signals:** Every claim backed by measurement; uncertainty always stated (+/-1sigma or +/-2sigma)

---

## Environmental Science & Technology (ES&T)

- **Tone:** Direct, policy-aware; environmental implications foregrounded
- **Sentences:** Medium length (20-30 words); active voice preferred
- **Macro logic:** Environmental problem -> Mechanistic insight -> Policy/management implications
- **Signature phrases:** "Our findings demonstrate...", "These results have important implications for..."
- **Opening rule:** First paragraph must establish broader societal impact
- **Signals:** Methods are secondary to significance; plain language valued

---

## Nature / Nature Geoscience

- **Tone:** Accessible to broad scientific audience; novelty and significance emphasized
- **Sentences:** Mix of short punchy and long complex; deliberate rhythm
- **Macro logic:** Big picture -> Knowledge gap -> This work -> Far-reaching significance
- **Signature phrases:** "Here we show...", "Our results reveal...", "This work establishes..."
- **Introduction:** 2-3 tight paragraphs; final sentence states the core finding
- **Signals:** Storytelling matters; avoid jargon in opening sentences

---

## Science / Science Advances

- **Tone:** Broad accessibility; emphasis on conceptual advance over methodology
- **Sentences:** Short to medium; active voice dominant; first person "we" common
- **Macro logic:** Context -> Question -> Key result -> Broader implication
- **Signature phrases:** "We find that...", "These data suggest...", "Our analysis reveals..."
- **Introduction:** Concise, narrative-driven; sets up a clear "question" by paragraph 2
- **Signals:** Figures carry the narrative; text guides interpretation of figures

---

## Science of the Total Environment

- **Tone:** Applied, management-oriented; explicitly links findings to policy/remediation
- **Sentences:** Clear and concise; direct statements preferred
- **Macro logic:** Problem -> Methodology -> Results -> Management implications
- **Signals:** Numerical data prominent; comparative analysis across sites

---

## Chemical Geology

- **Tone:** Analytical, methodology-heavy; precision over brevity
- **Macro logic:** Geological context -> Analytical approach -> Geochemical data -> Petrogenetic interpretation
- **Signals:** Instrument details and detection limits always reported

---

## Earth-Science Reviews

- **Tone:** Authoritative, synthesis-oriented; broad temporal and spatial scope
- **Sentences:** Long, information-dense; extensive use of subordinate clauses
- **Macro logic:** Historical perspective -> Current understanding -> Synthesis -> Future directions
- **Signals:** Comprehensive citation density; figures often schematic/conceptual

---

## Water Research

- **Tone:** Technical but solution-oriented; engineering perspective
- **Sentences:** Medium length; balanced active/passive
- **Macro logic:** Water quality problem -> Treatment approach -> Performance data -> Practical implications
- **Signals:** Removal efficiency (%), kinetic parameters, comparative performance tables

## General IMRaD Patterns

### Introduction
- Funnel structure: broad context -> narrow research problem
- Gap: "However, little is known about..." / "To date, no study has..."
- Objective: "The aim of this study was to..." / "Here, we investigated..."

### Methods
- Past passive: "Samples were collected..." / "Analysis was performed..."
- Always report: precision, detection limits, replicates ("in triplicate", "+/-1sigma")

### Results
- Data before interpretation
- Format: "X +/- Y (n=Z)" / "ranged from A to B"
- Cross-reference: "(Fig. 1)" / "(Table 2)"

### Discussion
- Comparison: "Consistent with previous studies..." / "In contrast to..."
- Uncertainty: "This may be attributed to..." / "One possible explanation is..."
- Significance: "These findings suggest that..." / "This has implications for..."
